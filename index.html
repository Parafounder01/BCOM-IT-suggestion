Semester I

Sem I · Core · 4 Credits
Financial Accounting I
What Is This?

Financial Accounting is the language of business. It teaches you how to record every financial transaction a company makes — from buying supplies to selling products — and summarize it in reports like Profit & Loss statements and Balance Sheets. Think of it as learning to keep a detailed, accurate diary of where money comes from and where it goes.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Every accountant, auditor, business analyst, and finance professional needs this skill. Companies like TCS, Deloitte, KPMG, and every bank test accounting fundamentals in their placement exams.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Practice journal entries daily — they are the foundation. Use the rule "Debit what comes in, Credit what goes out" as your anchor. Solve at least one complete final accounts problem with adjustments every week.

Journal Ledger Trial Balance Bank Reconciliation Depreciation Royalty
Sem I · Core · 4 Credits
Principles of Management
What Is This?

This subject explains how organizations work — how managers plan, organize, staff, lead, and control. You study ideas from great management thinkers like Taylor, Fayol, Drucker, and Mayo. It's like learning the rules of chess before you play the game.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Management principles are tested in every MBA entrance, bank PO exam, and company interview. Understanding how businesses are organized helps you work effectively in any team from day one.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Create a one-page cheat sheet of management theories with the author's name and key idea. Relate each concept to real companies you know — for example, "Amazon uses MBO for delivery targets." Practice writing 3-mark and 5-mark answers.

Planning Organizing Staffing MBO Motivation Controlling
Sem I · Elective · 3 Credits
Business Communication
What Is This?

This course trains you to write professional emails, reports, application letters, and speak confidently in public. It covers everything from drafting a complaint letter to writing a winning resume. Think of it as your transition from college writing to workplace writing.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Communication skills are tested in every job interview — group discussions, email writing tasks, and presentations. Your resume is the first thing a recruiter sees; this subject teaches you to make it count.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Practice writing one business letter per day (enquiry, complaint, job application). Record yourself giving a 2-minute speech and watch it back to improve. Build your actual resume as you learn the format.

Business Letters Report Writing Resume Interview Skills Public Speech
Sem I · Elective · 3 Credits
Business Economics
What Is This?

Economics is the study of choices — how people decide what to buy, how companies decide what to produce, and how prices are set. You'll learn about demand & supply, production costs, and different market types (monopoly, competition). It explains why a movie ticket costs ₹250 while an iPhone costs ₹80,000.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Bank PO exams, MBA entrances, and analytics roles heavily test economic reasoning. Every business decision is an economic decision — this subject trains you to think like a manager.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Draw demand-supply graphs repeatedly — visual memory helps. Relate concepts to everyday examples (e.g., "why does Uber cost more during rain?" = price elasticity). Practice numerical problems on elasticity and production.

Demand & Supply Elasticity Indifference Curve Production Function Market Structures
Sem I · NME · 2 Credits
Accounting for Business
What Is This?

A foundational crash course in accounting for students who may not have studied commerce before. You learn the essentials — what is debit/credit, how to prepare a cash book, and how to create simple final accounts. It's Accounting 101 in its simplest form.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Even if you come from a non-commerce background, this subject ensures you understand business numbers. It's helpful for banking, sales, and operations roles where financial literacy is expected.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Focus on the cash book and trial balance — they are the most practical tools. Solve each problem step by step rather than skipping ahead. Use colored pens for debit (left) and credit (right) columns.

Accounting Principles Journal Ledger Cash Book Final Accounts
Sem I · FC · 2 Credits
Office Fundamentals Lab
What Is This?

A hands-on lab where you master MS-Word, MS-Excel, MS-PowerPoint, and MS-Access. From creating professional documents and spreadsheets to designing presentations and databases — this is the digital toolkit every office worker needs. It's like learning to drive before you get on the road.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Excel proficiency is tested in almost every placement exam. Advanced Word and PowerPoint skills make you productive from day one at any company. Database basics in Access give you a head start on SQL.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Learn Excel shortcuts (Ctrl+Arrow, Alt+= for SUM). Build a mini-project — create a mark sheet with formulas and a chart. Learn mail merge in Word — it's a common interview question. Practice database queries in Access.

MS-Word MS-Excel MS-PowerPoint MS-Access Mail Merge

Semester II

Sem II · Core · 4 Credits
Financial Accounting II
What Is This?

Building on Accounting I, this subject covers advanced topics — hire purchases, branch operations, partnership accounts, and international standards (IFRS). You learn how to handle complex business situations like when a partner leaves or when a business dissolves. It's the next level of business fluency.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Partnership accounting is essential for CA firms and client accounting roles. IFRS knowledge is highly valued in Big 4 firms (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC) and multinational corporations.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Master the Garner vs Murray rule for partnership insolvency — it's a classic exam problem. Create comparison charts for different accounting methods. Read one IFRS standard summary each week to build global awareness.

Hire Purchase Branch Accounts Partnership Dissolution IFRS Ind AS
Sem II · Core · 4 Credits
Business Law
What Is This?

This subject teaches the legal rules that govern business — how contracts are formed, what makes an agreement legally valid, and your rights when buying or selling goods. It's like learning the rulebook of the business world.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Every business deal is a contract. Bank PO exams, company secretary roles, and legal compliance jobs heavily test business law. Knowing your legal rights protects you professionally and personally.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Learn the essential elements of a valid contract as a checklist: offer, acceptance, consideration, capacity, free consent, lawful object. Create flashcards for important sections (Section 2(a), 2(h), 10). Read one court case summary per week.

Indian Contract Act Offer & Acceptance Consideration Free Consent Sale of Goods Act
Sem II · Elective · 3 Credits
Introduction to Information Technology
What Is This?

This course demystifies how computers work — from binary numbers and hardware to networks and the internet. You learn how data travels across the globe in milliseconds and how different parts of a computer communicate. It's the "how things work" guide to the digital world.

Why It Matters For Your Career

This is what distinguishes a B.Com (IT) graduate from a regular B.Com graduate. IT services companies test basic computer awareness in their online tests. Networking knowledge helps in IT support, analytics, and fintech roles.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Learn to convert binary to decimal in your head — it's easier than it looks. Draw network topologies (star, bus, ring) — visual diagrams help. Practice one round of computer awareness MCQs daily.

Binary Hardware Fiber Optics LAN / WAN Internet Security
Sem II · Elective · 3 Credits
Informatics and Cyber Laws
What Is This?

This subject explores how IT is used in medicine, business, and artificial intelligence, and the legal framework governing the digital world — cybercrimes, privacy, and the IT Act 2000. It's your guide to staying safe and legal online.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Cyber law is a rapidly growing field. Companies need compliance professionals who understand data privacy. IT services and banking roles include cyber awareness sections in their tests.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Follow one cyber news story each week (data breaches, new regulations). Learn the key sections of the IT Act 2000 (Sections 43, 66, 67). Understand the difference between hacking, phishing, and identity theft.

Informatics Cybercrimes IT Act 2000 Data Privacy AI & VR
Sem II · NME · 2 Credits
Accounting for Decision Making
What Is This?

This subject takes accounting beyond bookkeeping into strategy — how managers use financial data to make decisions about pricing, production, and budgets. You'll learn break-even analysis, budgeting, and standard costing. It's accounting as a tool for thinking, not just recording.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Directly tested in MBA interviews, finance internships, and analyst roles. The ability to read a financial statement and make a recommendation separates a clerk from a manager.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Master the break-even point formula (BEP = Fixed Cost ÷ Contribution per unit) — it appears in every exam. Practice comparative and common-size balance sheets. Create a budget for your own monthly expenses to understand the concept intuitively.

Management Accounting Ratio Analysis Marginal Costing Standard Costing Budgetary Control
Sem II · SEC · 2 Credits
C Programming Lab
What Is This?

A hands-on lab where you learn to write C programs — from simple interest calculations to sorting numbers and checking palindromes. You learn how to think like a programmer: break problems down into logical steps and write instructions a computer can follow.

Why It Matters For Your Career

Programming fundamentals are tested in IT company placement exams (TCS NQT, Infosys BPM). Even if you don't become a developer, understanding code helps you work better with tech teams.

Simple Tips To Ace This

Practice writing the same program multiple times from memory. Focus on loops (for, while) and arrays — they are the backbone of most programs. Use a C compiler app on your phone to practice during commute time.

Loops Arrays Sorting Palindrome File Handling
